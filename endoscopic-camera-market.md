<p><p>An endoscopic camera is a medical device used during endoscopic procedures to capture images and video of the internal structures of the body. This growth can be attributed to the increasing prevalence of gastrointestinal diseases, respiratory disorders, and cancer, which are driving the demand for endoscopic procedures. Technological advancements in endoscopic cameras, such as the development of high-definition cameras with better image quality and improved ergonomics, are also contributing to market growth.</p><p>One of the latest trends in the endoscopic camera market is the increasing adoption of wireless and capsule endoscopy. Wireless endoscopic cameras offer greater flexibility and maneuverability during procedures, while capsule endoscopy allows for non-invasive visualization of the gastrointestinal tract. These innovations are expected to drive market growth in the coming years as healthcare providers seek more efficient and patient-friendly diagnostic tools.</p></p>
