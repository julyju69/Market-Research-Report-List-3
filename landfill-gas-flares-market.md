<p><p>Landfill gas flares come in various types, including candlestick flares and enclosed ground-flares. Candlestick flares are vertical, open structures designed to burn off landfill gas, while enclosed ground-flares are covered systems that combust gas underground. Both types of flares are used to control emissions of methane and other gases produced by decaying waste in landfills. These systems help to reduce air pollution and minimize the environmental impact of landfill gas emissions.</p></p>
