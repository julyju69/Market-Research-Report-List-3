<p><p>Touch sensors are widely used in various devices, with two main types dominating the market: resistive and capacitive sensors. Resistive touch sensors work by sensing pressure applied to the screen, making them suitable for stylus input. On the other hand, capacitive touch sensors detect changes in electrical fields caused by the touch of a finger, offering a more responsive and durable option for modern touchscreen devices. Both types have their own advantages and applications, driving innovation and competition in the touch sensor market.</p></p>
