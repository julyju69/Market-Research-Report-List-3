<p><ul><li>Resonant Membrane</li><li>Periodic Resonators</li><li>Sonic Crystal</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Acoustic metamaterials can be classified into three main types: resonant membrane, periodic resonators, and sonic crystal. Resonant membrane metamaterials use thin membranes to alter acoustic wave propagation. Periodic resonators create band gaps in sound waves through a pattern of repeating structures. Sonic crystal metamaterials consist of a periodic arrangement of resonators that control the movement of acoustic waves. Each type of metamaterial offers unique properties for manipulating and controlling sound waves in various applications.</p></p>
