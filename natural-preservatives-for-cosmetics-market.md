<p><p>Natural preservatives for cosmetics can be categorized into various types based on their chemical composition. Lipids, such as oils and fats, can act as preservatives by creating a barrier that prevents microbial growth. Acids, like citric acid and sorbic acid, are effective at lowering the pH levels in products, inhibiting the growth of bacteria and fungi. Alcohols, such as ethanol and benzyl alcohol, work by dehydrating microbes and preventing their multiplication. Other types of natural preservatives include plant extracts, essential oils, and antimicrobial peptides.</p></p>
