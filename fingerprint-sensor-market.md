<p><p>Fingerprint sensors are available in three main types: capacitive, optical, and thermal. Capacitive sensors use an electrical current to capture fingerprint images, offering high accuracy and reliability. Optical sensors rely on light to scan fingerprints, providing a quick and easy way to authenticate users. Thermal sensors detect temperature differences on the skin's surface, capturing unique fingerprint patterns. Each type of sensor has its own strengths and weaknesses, making them suitable for different applications in the biometric security market.</p></p>
