<p><p>V2I (Vehicle-to-Infrastructure) communication systems enable vehicles to communicate with infrastructure such as traffic lights and road signs, improving traffic flow and safety. V2X (Vehicle-to-Everything) systems allow vehicles to communicate with other vehicles, infrastructure, pedestrians, and networks, facilitating advanced safety features and autonomous driving capabilities. V2P (Vehicle-to-Pedestrian) systems focus on communication between vehicles and pedestrians to enhance safety in urban environments. Other types of vehicular communication systems include V2G (Vehicle-to-Grid) and V2H (Vehicle-to-Home), which enable vehicles to interact with energy grids and home appliances.</p></p>
