<p><p>Terahertz spectroscopy utilizes radiation in the terahertz frequency range, between infrared and microwave wavelengths, to analyze materials. Mid-infrared spectroscopy operates in the middle of the infrared spectrum, providing detailed information about molecular structure. Near-infrared spectroscopy uses shorter wavelengths than mid-infrared, useful for analyzing organic compounds. Far-infrared spectroscopy covers longer wavelengths than mid-infrared, typically used for studying lattice vibrations in solids. Each type of spectroscopy offers unique insights into the properties and composition of materials, making them valuable tools in scientific research and industry.</p></p>
