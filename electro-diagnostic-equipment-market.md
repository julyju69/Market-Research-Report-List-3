<p><ul><li>Electromyogram (EMG)</li><li>The Nerve Conduction (NCS)</li><li>Electrocardiography (ECG)</li><li>Electroencephalography (EEG)</li><li>Electroretinography (ERG)</li><li>Electrogastrography (EGG)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Electromyogram (EMG) equipment is used to measure the electrical activity of muscles, while Nerve Conduction (NCS) devices assess the function of nerves by measuring the speed of electrical signals. Electrocardiography (ECG) equipment measures the electrical activity of the heart to diagnose heart conditions. Electroencephalography (EEG) devices monitor brain activity by recording electrical signals. Electroretinography (ERG) equipment evaluates the function of the retina, while Electrogastrography (EGG) devices measure the electrical activity of the stomach for gastrointestinal assessments.</p></p>
