<p><p>Functional flours are categorized into two main types – cereals and legumes. Cereal-based functional flours are derived from grains like wheat, rice, corn, and oats, known for their high fiber and protein content. On the other hand, legume-based functional flours are made from beans, lentils, and chickpeas, which are rich in plant-based proteins and nutrients. Both types of functional flours are gaining popularity in the market due to their health benefits and versatility in various food products.</p></p>
