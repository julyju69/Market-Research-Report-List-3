<p><p>Viral vectors are widely used in gene therapy and vaccine development. Retroviral vectors integrate their genetic material into the host cell genome, making them stable for long-term gene expression. Lentiviral vectors are derived from HIV and can infect both dividing and non-dividing cells. Adenoviral vectors are efficient at delivering genes to a wide range of cell types. Herpes simplex virus vectors have a large cargo capacity for gene delivery. Chimeric viral vectors combine characteristics of different viruses for improved gene delivery. Other viral vectors include adeno-associated viruses and poxviruses.</p></p>
