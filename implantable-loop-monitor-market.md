<p><p>An Implantable Loop Monitor (ILM) is a small device that is implanted just beneath the skin in the chest area to continuously monitor the heart's electrical activity for extended periods of time. This growth can be attributed to the increasing prevalence of cardiovascular diseases, rise in geriatric population, technological advancements in monitoring devices, and growing awareness about the importance of early detection of heart conditions. Additionally, the rising adoption of remote monitoring solutions and the availability of reimbursement policies for cardiac monitoring devices are also driving the market growth.</p><p>Some of the latest trends in the Implantable Loop Monitor market include the development of smaller and more discreet devices, integration of artificial intelligence for accurate diagnosis, and the increasing use of telemedicine for remote monitoring of patients.
