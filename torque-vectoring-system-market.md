<p><p>Torque vectoring systems are available in three main types based on the vehicle's drivetrain configuration: Front Wheel Drive (FWD), Rear Wheel Drive (RWD), and All Wheel Drive/Four Wheel Drive (4WD). FWD vehicles allocate torque between the front wheels to improve traction and stability. RWD vehicles distribute torque between the rear wheels for better acceleration and handling. 4WD vehicles can vary torque distribution between all four wheels to enhance performance and off-road capability. Each system offers unique benefits tailored to different driving conditions and preferences.</p></p>
