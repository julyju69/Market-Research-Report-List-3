<p><p>Sound insulation plasterboards are available in two main types - tapered-edged and square-edged. Tapered-edged plasterboards have recessed edges, making it easier to fill and finish joints, resulting in a seamless finish. On the other hand, square-edged plasterboards have straight edges, requiring more attention to achieve a smooth finish. Both types provide sound insulation properties, with tapered-edged boards being preferred for their ease of installation and overall aesthetics, while square-edged boards are chosen for specific applications where a square finish is desired.</p></p>
